Does Paying More for College Result in More Earnings?

by John Dambra

This narrative visualization explores the relationship between the cost of various colleges to the future earnings of graduates from the colleges. The visuals show the relationship between tuition cost and future earnings by plotting the values of various colleges and fitting a linear line of best fit. The slope of this line can help us determine the value of an extra $1 spent in terms of generating additional future earnings. Specifically, this visualization will explore differences in Public, Private, and Proprietary College types to determine if one type of school has more value as measured by generating more future income from any additional tuition cost spent. The below graph shows all the colleges in the data set. As mentioned, a linear line of best fit is included to show approximately how much more money a graduate can hope to earn for each additional dollar spent. In this case, the slope of the line is $.44. This means that for each additional dollar spent on tuition, a graduate can expect to earn approximately $.44 a year in future earnings.
